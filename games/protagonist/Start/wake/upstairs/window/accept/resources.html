Adventures of The Protagonist.

You duck, dodging the first bolt as your eyes quickly dart
around the room, looking for anything you can use to your
advantage. The room is completely bare, nothing decorates the
blank, bare walls except... no. Disgusting. Anything but that.
But it seems you have no choice. As the alien takes another
shot you dive against the wall, tearing the glaring eyeball out
of it's mechanical socket. It pulses in your hand as you throw
it with all your might at the hostile. It explodes on impact.
Sparks fly everywhere as the alien and the eye dissolve. A
compartment opens up, fashioned with only a single red button.
You have no option but to push it. You hear a clank, then a
BANG as a door opens and you are sucked into space. The giant
universe that surrounds you takes your breath away, your eyes
widen as you're exposed to the wonderous nebulas and galaxies
of outer space. You never realised how enormously huge the
universe really was, and in effect how extremely small you really
are. You slowly realize the futility in your existence. You
You realize how meaningless you are, how meaningless everything
is. Nothing you will ever do in your pointless little life will
ever amount to anything. In an aimless universe with no goal, how
can anything you do be important? And even if there is some mystical
purpose to reality, you have no idea what that is and you never will.
But the odds are that there isn't an answer. Nothing you can do will change the
fact that literally nothing matters. But you also realize that even
the fact that nothing matters doesn't matter. And why worry about
something that doesn't matter? It doesn't matter that you are
nothing. No one ever said you had to be something. You are useless,
but that's fine. Who ever said you need to have a use? You can ask
why you shouldn't just give up. If nothing matters, why do anything?
The answer is because there's nothing else to do. Why do people build
castles in the sand when the know that it will be swept away by
the tide the very next day? Why start up a sandbox game with no end
goal? Why wake up every morning to a meaningless existence? Because
it's something to do. Because even if it's true that nothing matters,
at least you know you did your best. It sucks, but giving up is illogical
and therefore wrong. Giving up accomplishes nothing. Trying, even if
it is meaningless, accomplishes at least something.You angle yourself
to float back into the ship, grab the handle and pull yourself back inside.
