Adventures of The Protagonist.

You thrash around trying to grab onto something, anything,
to pull yourself back. You grab the edge of your desk and
clamp your hands down. The tractor beam pulls at you,
it's strength increasing. Your muscles clench, trying to
hold on. Eventually it becomes too much and your left arm
gives out. You take a breath and try again, grabbing the
window curtain. Your other hand gives. You're whole body
is outside of the window now, your left hand barely holding
the curtains. The intensity of the tractor beam increases
further, and you watch as the curtain's fabric slowly tears.
With a final rip, the fabric breaks and you find yourself
zooming towards the object. You fly across the sky, desperately
trying to grab anything you can. Tree branches, rooftop
chimneys, anything. At last, you grab at the gutter of a
house and barely hold on. You pour all your energy into
staying in place, you are utterly exhausted. Powered only
by adrenaline and the fear of whatever is on that ship,
your eyes water and you clamp harder onto the gutter. You
gasp in oxygen, trying to give your body the energy it
needs to function. You scream, but no sound comes out. Your
vision blurs, and you grunt as it fades to darkness.

When you wake up, you're on the ground. You sit up,
and get a sharp pain in your side. Your head throbs,
you're exhausted, but you're alive, and you worked hard
to be that way. You wonder how long it's been since you
passed out. You look up at the stars, and realise your
purpose. The meaning of existence is working hard. It's
doing the best you can, and working towards your goals.
